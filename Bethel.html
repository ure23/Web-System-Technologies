There are lots of pieces to think about when creating a webpage.

1. Page Title

The page title should tell visitors exactly

  • Keep it short Use clear language

    • 2. ID

    The ID is a label assigned to the webpage

  • Use hyphens between words.

    • 3. Description

    The description is a one-sentence summary of your page.

    Where your description might show up:

  • Google search results page

    • 4. Body

    The body is the bulk of your webpage content.

  • Put your information in priority order with the most important information first.

    • 5. Headings

    Headings help users scan and find information

  • Accurately describe the information in the section.

    • When formatting your headings.

    6. Lists

    A list is a helpful tool for breaking down

  • Start with a capital letter.

    • 7. Bold, Italics, and Underline

    On the web, bold, italics, and underline each have a unique purpose.

    Bold

    Use bold for highlighting important info

    Italics

    Use italics according to the Bethel Style Guide:

  • Book titles
  • Movie titles

    • Italics can also be used to consistently

    Examples:

  • Job titles on a staff list
    • Underline

    Never underline text on the web.

    8. Links

    Hyperlink meaningful phrases rather than single words.

  • Phrases are easier to spot.
  • Avoid using "here" and "click here."

    • 9. Call to Action

    Nearly all webpages should provide visitors with a call to action.
